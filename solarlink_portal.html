SolarLink / Why Solar?
Why Solar in 2026?
You don't need to become a solar expert. You just need to understand why a homeowner might care — and know when to say "let me introduce you to someone."
"Homes need electricity. That doesn't change. The only question is how you want to pay for it over the next 25 years."
Module 1 — Why Solar Now
  • Electricity costs have risen 5–7% per year historically
  • AI and EVs are driving electricity demand higher
  • Florida hurricanes keep exposing grid vulnerability
  • Solar lets the roof become productive instead of just sitting there
Module 2 — How Solar Works
Your roof produces power during the day. Your home uses it first. Extra energy goes back to the grid for credits. The home stays connected to the utility. A Tesla Powerwall battery adds backup power during outages. The goal: lower net monthly cost, better protection, long-term savings.
Most homes stay connected to the utility. This isn't off-grid — it's about producing power right where it's needed.
Module 3 — Why Solar Isn't For Everyone
Some homes are great fits. Some aren't. That is why SolarLink uses a Personalized Solar Overview — so the homeowner gets an honest answer based on their specific roof, usage, and location.
"Good solar feels logical. Bad solar feels forced."
Module 4 — What Makes a Good Referral
Homeowner (owns the property)

Open to a no-pressure conversation

Utility bill available to upload
Module 5 — Common Homeowner Questions
Is solar free?+
Not exactly. For qualified homeowners there is typically no large upfront deposit — but the system does have a cost that is structured over time per agreement. Never say "free solar."
What if I move?+
The agreement is designed to transfer with the home — similar to an electric bill. The next homeowner can assume the same lower power cost structure.
What about during an outage?+
Without a battery, solar panels stop during a grid outage for safety reasons. A Tesla Powerwall battery adds backup power that recharges from the sun after the storm.
What if my roof is old?+
If the roof isn't in solid condition, Alex will let the homeowner know before moving forward. A good solar pro should say no when the situation isn't right.
What if they already have solar?+
Still worth submitting — they may be eligible for a battery addition, a system upgrade, or be paying more than they should be under an older agreement.
Module 6 — Why Referrals Work Better Than Ads
People trust people more than ads, call centers, or random door knockers. A homeowner is far more likely to take a meeting when the introduction comes from someone they already know and trust. That's the whole point of SolarLink. You are the trusted layer.
